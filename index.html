With the renovation of wind turbine, DkIT are happy to invest in the green future of the campus. The famous turbine is representative of the DkIT concept of ' living-learning campus. The turbine plays a key role in work at the Center for Renewable Energy at DkIT (CREDIT) and teaching activities at the School of Engineering, in addition to providing large green energy and cost-saving contributions to our Institute.

At the time of construction, the turbine of this size and power was a "world first" for an academic campus with a hub height of 60 m , a rotor diameter of 52 m and each blade is 26m.

Now a familiar icon in the North Leinster South Ulster region, the turbine has had a strong local influence with frequent site visits from a large proportion of company, interest groups also the primary and secondary schools. It was also adopted as part of the Dundalk icon by the Dundalk Town Council.

It has cost about €1 million to build the windturbine
